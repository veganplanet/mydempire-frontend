MydEmpire — Genesis FAQ

Questions and answers about packs, land, blueprints, EMP, treasury and reward mechanics in the MydEmpire Genesis strategy economy.

What is MydEmpire? overview
›

MydEmpire is a Web3 industrial strategy economy built on the Hive blockchain. Players purchase Genesis Packs, receive Land, Blueprints and EMP, build factories, generate Empire Power (EP), and earn treasury-backed rewards.

It is a structured economic simulation — not a yield-farming system or fixed-return product.

What does a Genesis Pack contain? packs
›

Each Genesis Pack contains:

  • 1 Land NFT
  • 1 Blueprint NFT
  • EMP (Empire Monetary Power)
  • A small chance of an Imperial Relic

When you open a Genesis Pack, the pack NFT is burned permanently. Genesis supply is capped at 5000 packs.

What are Land tiers? land
›

Genesis Land comes in three tiers:

  • L1 — Frontier (1 factory slot)
  • L2 — Estate (2 factory slots)
  • L3 — Industrial Zone (3 factory slots)

Higher-tier land allows more factories and stronger production potential. Land is the base layer for your industrial network.

What is a Blueprint? blueprints
›

Blueprints determine industry type and factory level (B1–B5). They are consumed when you construct a factory on your land.

Different levels represent different production strength, and drop probabilities are weighted to keep the economy balanced.

I own L2 or L3 land but don't have enough Blueprints. What can I do? slot fill
›

If you own higher-tier land (L2 or L3) and do not have enough blueprints to use all available factory slots, you may mint additional blueprints using EMP.

Rules:

  • Eligibility applies only to L2 and L3 land owners.
  • Minting is allowed only if your total blueprints are fewer than your total available land slots.
  • Blueprint level probabilities are identical to Genesis pack blueprint drop rates.
  • EMP spent for blueprint minting is allocated 100% to the treasury.

This prevents land slots from remaining permanently idle and strengthens the long-term economic system.

What is EMP? emp
›

EMP (Empire Monetary Power) is the internal utility resource of MydEmpire. It is used for:

  • Building factories
  • Upgrading factories
  • Blueprint minting (for L2/L3 land owners)
  • Future strategic mechanics

EMP interacts with the treasury and functions as a key part of the closed-loop economic system.

What is EP? rewards
›

EP (Empire Power) represents your economic weight in the system. Rewards are distributed proportionally based on your share of total EP.

Rewards are funded by the treasury, not by unlimited token inflation or fixed APY promises.

What is a Genesis WRIT? writ
›

A Genesis WRIT is a special strategic NFT issued during the Genesis Era. It represents an imperial charter granted to early supporters of MydEmpire.

Each WRIT provides +1 flat Empire Power (EP), requires no maintenance, and is always active.

Unlike Imperial Relics, WRITs do not give percentage-based boosts or compounding multipliers. They are designed as stable base EP contributors that support long-term balance instead of explosive growth.

What is an Imperial Relic? relics
›

Imperial Relics are ultra-rare Genesis NFTs that provide strategic boosts:

  • Very low drop chance per pack
  • +3% EP boost per relic
  • Maximum 2 relics counted per player (6% cap)

They are Genesis-only prestige assets and part of the EP boost system.

How does revenue work? treasury
›

When a player purchases a pack, the HIVE payment flows into the revenue account and is then allocated as:

  • 50% → Treasury
  • 40% → DAO allocation
  • 10% → Founder allocation

Marketplace fees and EMP-based blueprint minting further reinforce the treasury, supporting long-term reward emissions.

How are rewards distributed? emission
›

Rewards are funded directly from the treasury and distributed proportionally based on each player's share of Empire Power (EP).

The system operates under a maximum daily emission cap of up to 0.5% of the treasury balance. Actual emission may be adjusted depending on treasury health, network conditions, and long-term sustainability parameters.

There are no fixed returns or guaranteed yields. Rewards depend on overall system activity, treasury strength, and competitive EP distribution.
