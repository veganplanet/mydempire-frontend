Genesis Era

A Colorful Strategy Economy on Hive

MydEmpire is a treasury-backed industrial strategy game on the Hive blockchain. Players open packs, build factories, generate Empire Power (EP), and compete for a dynamic reward stream that is constrained by real treasury health, not by unlimited token inflation.

1. The Core Economic Loop

At the heart of MydEmpire is a simple, closed economic loop:

Packs → Land & Blueprints & EMP → Factories → EP → Treasury-backed Rewards → Marketplace → Treasury

Players do not “farm yield” from a printer. Instead, they build industrial networks that compete for a share of a finite, treasury-backed emission. The system is designed to be understandable, transparent and durable.

2. Core Assets and Their Roles

The Genesis Era focuses on a small set of carefully connected assets:

  • PACK — Sealed asset that, when opened, burns and generates land, a blueprint, EMP and a small chance of an Imperial Relic.
  • LAND — Defines how many factories you can build. Comes in three tiers (L1, L2, L3) with 1–3 slots.
  • BLUEPRINT — Defines industry type and factory level (B1–B5). Consumed when a factory is constructed.
  • FACTORY — The built asset. Consumes EMP for upgrades and generates EP over time.
  • EMP (Empire Monetary Power) — Internal utility resource used for building, upgrading and blueprint minting for extra land slots.
  • EP (Empire Power) — Your economic weight in the system, used to determine reward share.
  • Imperial Relic — Ultra-rare boost NFT that increases your EP by a fixed percentage within a capped limit.

Future eras may introduce additional layers, but Genesis intentionally focuses on a small, tightly controlled set of moving parts.

3. Genesis Packs and Supply

Genesis is capped at a fixed number of packs. Each pack is a sealed NFT that:

  • Is purchased using HIVE.
  • Is held or opened at the player’s choice.
  • Burns permanently when opened.
  • Generates exactly one Land, one Blueprint, EMP and a small chance of a Relic.

This limited pack supply forms the foundation of the entire Genesis economic landscape. There is no hidden secondary mint or silent supply expansion.

4. Land Tiers and Production Slots

Land is the canvas on which your industrial empire is built. Each piece of land provides a fixed number of factory slots:

  • L1 — Frontier • 1 factory slot
  • L2 — Estate • 2 factory slots
  • L3 — Industrial Zone • 3 factory slots

The purpose of land is not to speculate on maps, but to define your maximum industrial capacity. More slots means more factories, more EP, and a higher potential share of rewards — if you choose to use them.

5. Blueprint System and Factory Levels

Blueprints define what you build and how strong it is. Each blueprint has:

  • An industry type (Food, Textile, Pharma, Chemical, Supermarket).
  • A level from B1 to B5, where higher levels represent more powerful factories.

When you construct a factory on a land slot, the corresponding blueprint is consumed and the factory becomes a persistent asset in the system.

Higher level factories contribute more EP, but may require more EMP or more careful planning to build and maintain.

6. EMP: Utility Resource and Structural Sink

EMP (Empire Monetary Power) is a non-speculative utility resource used inside the MydEmpire economy. Its primary roles include:

  • Paying construction costs for new factories.
  • Upgrading existing factories to higher effectiveness levels.
  • Minting additional blueprints to fill unused L2/L3 land slots (see the dedicated Blueprint Mint page).

EMP is earned through packs and production, then partially removed from circulation when spent on actions that feed value back towards the treasury or game economy. This helps prevent runaway liquidity build-up with no purpose.

7. Slot Utilization for L2 and L3 Land

Because L2 and L3 land provide multiple slots, it is possible for a player to own land capacity without enough blueprints to fill it. To avoid idle production capacity, the system allows:

  • Eligibility-based blueprint minting for L2 and L3 owners whose total blueprints are fewer than their total land slots.
  • Each additional blueprint is minted by paying EMP and generated using the same level probabilities as pack-based blueprints.
  • EMP spent on this minting is allocated 100% to the treasury.

This mechanism converts “dead slots” into active factories while reinforcing treasury sustainability. A full technical description is available on the Blueprint Mint Mechanism page.

8. Empire Power (EP) and Reward Allocation

EP (Empire Power) measures the strength of your industrial network. It is derived from:

  • Your land and factory configuration.
  • Factory levels and upgrades.
  • Active boosts from rare assets such as Imperial Relics.

Rewards are funded directly from the treasury and distributed proportionally based on each player’s share of total EP. In other words, if you hold 2% of the network’s EP, you are eligible for roughly 2% of the day’s emission, subject to caps and adjustments.

The system operates under a maximum daily emission cap of up to 0.5% of the treasury balance. Actual emission may be adjusted depending on treasury health, network conditions, and long-term sustainability parameters.

There are no fixed yields or guaranteed returns. Rewards are a byproduct of productive participation inside the system, not a promise.

9. Treasury, Revenue Split and Resilience

When players buy packs, HIVE flows into a revenue account and is allocated using a transparent rule:

  • 50% → Protocol Treasury (funds reward emissions)
  • 40% → DAO allocation (for future governance decisions)
  • 10% → Founder allocation (to support development)

Additional inflows, such as marketplace fees or EMP sinks, can also reinforce the treasury. Because rewards are always a percentage of the treasury, not an arbitrary token print, the system has built-in resilience: emissions can slow automatically if the treasury weakens.

10. Fairness, Boost Caps and Anti-Whale Design

MydEmpire includes several structural safeguards to keep the economy competitive without becoming a “winner takes all” system:

  • Boost caps — Rare items such as Imperial Relics provide EP boosts, but only up to a fixed maximum so that no single player can multiply their share indefinitely.
  • Finite land slots — Even with many packs, total productive capacity is limited by land slots, forcing strategic choices instead of pure volume dominance.
  • EMP sinks — Large players who wish to push further often spend more EMP, which ultimately strengthens the treasury.

The result is a competitive field where scale matters, but smart planning and timing also carry significant weight.

11. Beyond Genesis: A Multi-Era Economy

Genesis is intentionally simple and controlled. Future eras may expand the system in stages:

  • Expansion Era — Deeper industrial chains and resource layers.
  • Industrial Era — Advanced factories and complex synergies.
  • Sovereign Era — Governance and DAO-directed parameters.
  • Dominion Era — Long-term endgame systems and prestige structures.

Each phase will build on the same core principle: a colorful variety of strategic options inside a disciplined, treasury-backed economic frame.
