📦 Factory Goods Guide

How Goods Work in MydEmpire

Goods are the product layer of MydEmpire. Your active factories can produce collectible products, give them Product Value, and submit them into dynamic redemption cycles for EMP pool rewards.

1. What are Goods?

Goods are finished products produced by active factories inside MydEmpire.

Factories do not only create EP/day. They can also create product items such as food baskets, textile items, pharma products, cleaning products, and supermarket goods.

Goods make your factories feel more alive because your empire now produces real collectible products, not only numbers.

2. How are Goods produced?

Goods are produced by your active factories. A factory must be built, active, ready for Goods claim, and inside your current Goods production capacity.

Factory status matters Inactive factories, factories under construction, or factories still in cooldown cannot produce Goods.
EP/day matters Higher EP/day unlocks more factory access and higher Goods processing limits.
Factory level matters Stronger factories can produce more Goods and have better chances for higher product levels and quality.
Cooldown matters Goods are claimed in claim cycles. After claiming, the next claim becomes available after cooldown.

3. Goods Production Capacity

Goods production uses two important limits: factory access and maximum Goods processing.

A. Factory Access Limit

This decides how many active factories can participate in Goods production.

EP/day Factory Access Meaning
Below 100 0% Goods locked
100–499 30% active factories Starter Production
500–999 75% active factories Expanded Production
1000+ 100% active factories Full Production
When factory access is limited, MydEmpire uses factory rotation. This means different eligible factories get their chance over time instead of the same factories always producing.

B. Max Goods Processing Limit

This decides the maximum number of Goods that can be received from one Goods claim.

EP/day Max Goods Processing Label
100–499 15 Goods Starter Processing
500–999 35 Goods Expanded Processing
1000–1999 60 Goods Advanced Processing
2000–4999 85 Goods Industrial Processing
5000+ 110 Goods Imperial Processing
These limits are balancing settings. MydEmpire may adjust the limits or add new EP/day slabs in the future if economy growth, player activity, or Goods balance requires it.

6. Product Value and Quality

Every Good has a Product Level and Quality. Together they decide the final Product Value, also called PV.

Product Level

Product Level Base PV
Essential 10 PV
Standard 20 PV
Value 30 PV
Premium 40 PV
Luxury 50 PV

Quality Multiplier

Quality Multiplier
Standard ×1
Fine ×2
Superior ×3
Base Product Value × Quality Multiplier = Final PV
Example: Premium Good = 40 base PV. Superior Quality = ×3. Final PV = 120 PV.

Higher-level factories have better chances to produce stronger product levels and better quality Goods.

7. Utility of Goods

Goods are mainly used in Goods Redemption Cycles. During an active cycle, players can submit selected Goods from inventory.

Submitted Goods Submitted Goods leave available inventory and enter the active redemption cycle.
Submitted PV The Product Value of submitted Goods is added to your cycle total.
Leaderboard Your position changes based on how much Product Value you submit.
Estimated EMP Your estimated EMP updates as the pool and total submitted PV change.
Submitted Goods cannot be returned to inventory.

8. Fortnightly Redemption Cycle

Goods Redemption is planned as a regular cycle, around every 14 days.

Each cycle has:

  • EMP Pool
  • Total Submitted PV
  • Live EMP per PV rate
  • Leaderboard
  • Estimated player rewards
EMP per PV = Cycle EMP Pool ÷ Total Submitted Product Value
Example: If the cycle EMP pool is 1,000 EMP and total submitted PV is 10,000 PV, then the live rate is 0.10 EMP per PV. If a player submitted 1,000 PV, the estimated reward is 100 EMP.

This rate can change until the cycle ends because more players may submit more Goods.

Redemption Leaderboard

During an active cycle, players are ranked by submitted Product Value. When a cycle ends, players can use View Previous Cycles to see the last 5 completed cycles.

9. Bi–Tri Monthly Special Goods Events

Apart from normal fortnightly cycles, MydEmpire may run larger Goods events every 2–3 months.

Event Type Meaning
Industry Rush One industry gets special focus.
Mega Redemption A larger EMP pool creates stronger competition.
Festival Cycle Special themed Goods become highlighted.
Premium Goods Event Higher PV Goods become more important.
Leaderboard Event Top players compete for extra recognition or bonus rewards.
Special events create excitement without replacing normal Goods redemption cycles.

10. Strategy Tips

Small Empire Reach 100 EP/day first to unlock Goods production.
Growing Empire Moving from 100–499 EP/day to 500–999 EP/day increases factory access from 30% to 75%.
Strong Empire At 1000+ EP/day, all active factories can participate in Goods production.
Industrial Empire At 2000+ EP/day, processing capacity becomes much stronger with up to 85 Goods.
Imperial Empire At 5000+ EP/day, the current highest processing slab allows up to 110 Goods.
Cycle Timing Watch the EMP pool, total submitted PV, live rate, and time left before submitting everything.

Important Rules

  • Goods unlock at 100 EP/day.
  • Goods production depends on active factories.
  • Factory access depends on EP/day.
  • Max Goods per claim depends on EP/day.
  • Higher factory levels can produce more Goods.
  • Higher factory levels have better product level and quality chances.
  • Goods must be submitted into redemption cycles to compete.
  • Submitted Goods cannot be returned.
  • Final EMP depends on the cycle pool and total submitted PV.
  • Goods limits may be adjusted later for balancing if required.

Simple Summary

Build factories, increase EP/day, unlock more Goods capacity, claim Goods, collect better products, submit Goods into redemption cycles, and compete by Product Value for the EMP pool.
