Slot Utilization Blueprint Mint Mechanism

Formal description of how L2 and L3 land owners may mint additional blueprints using EMP and how this reinforces long-term treasury sustainability.

1. Structural Purpose

Genesis L2 and L3 lands provide multiple production slots, while each Genesis Pack generates a single blueprint. This creates a natural slot–blueprint mismatch.

To prevent idle production capacity and preserve economic efficiency, MydEmpire introduces the Slot Utilization Blueprint Mint Mechanism.

2. Eligibility Rules

A player may mint additional blueprints only if:

  • They own L2 (Estate) or L3 (Industrial Zone) land.
  • Their total blueprint count is lower than their total available land slots.
  • EMP cost is paid for each mint.

This prevents uncontrolled blueprint farming and ensures structural fairness.

3. Blueprint Generation Logic

Blueprint minting uses the same weighted probabilities as Genesis pack drops.

No preferential bias is introduced. EMP minting respects the same rarity curve.

4. Treasury Allocation Rule

EMP spent on blueprint minting is allocated 100% to the treasury.

  • No DAO split.
  • No founder allocation.
  • No side-channel distribution.

This functions as a structural economic sink and strengthens reward sustainability.

5. Economic Function

The blueprint mint mechanism:

  • Prevents idle slot inefficiency
  • Balances blueprint supply
  • Reinforces treasury accumulation
  • Stabilizes EP growth
  • Supports long-term emission durability

It integrates directly into the closed-loop system:

Revenue → Treasury → Production → Rewards → Market → Treasury
